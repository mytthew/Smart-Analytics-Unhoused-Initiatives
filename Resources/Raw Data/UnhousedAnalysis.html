Data Limitations

Privacy
Privacy is always a concern when working with data. All data was anonymized so that no personal information was included in the dataset. Due to this, it proves difficult to track individuals over time.
Quality
When working with a large dataset that spans the nation and different organizations, there may not be a consistent method of collection.
Mobility
This population is very mobile and may not be counted in the same location each time. Point in time counts are not always accurate as there may not be sufficient staff for collection and topography can also be a barrier.
Sampling Bias
Barriers to entry may have caused a sampling bias. For example, if a person is not able to access a shelter, they may not be counted. Also, if a person does not speak the language or they do not want help, they may not be counted. People may not consider themselves homeless if they are couch surfing or living in a car.
Circumstances
People may not always be in a circumstance that would allow them to qualify for help in shelters, but also may not have a lifestyle that would allow for consistent/permanent residence status. Extenuating circumstances make it difficult to "label" someone's lifestyle and situation based on a handful of generalized demographics.
Time
Starting with the amount of time we had to work on this project, we were not able to dive as deep as we would have liked. We would have liked to have more time to explore the data and the possibilities of what we could do with it. Also, the data itself is limited by time. We only have data from 2007 to 2023.
